Chapter 48 — Durable Agentic Workflows

What You'll Learn​

Long-running AI agents can execute for minutes or hours. When they crash mid-way, every completed step is lost and the workflow must restart from scratch — expensive, slow, and unreliable. Durable workflows solve this by persisting state after every step, enabling automatic resume from exactly where execution stopped.

Prerequisites​

  • Chapter 20 (Agentic Loop) — you should understand how an agent reasons and acts in a loop
  • Chapter 23 (Planning and Task Decomposition) — you should understand multi-step task planning

What You'll Learn​

  • Why durability matters — the cost of restarting a long-running agent from scratch
  • Checkpointing — saving workflow state to disk or a database after every step
  • Resume on failure — reloading a checkpoint and continuing from the last completed step
  • Idempotency keys — ensuring a completed step is never re-executed accidentally
  • Step-level retry — retrying individual failed steps, not the entire workflow
  • Saga pattern — defining compensating (undo) actions for each step
  • Workflow engines — when and why to use Temporal, Prefect, LangGraph, or Celery
