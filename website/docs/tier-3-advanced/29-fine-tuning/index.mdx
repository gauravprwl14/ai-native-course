## Why This Matters

Prompt engineering is powerful, but it has limits. You can't prompt a model into always responding in a specific brand voice, always following a proprietary JSON schema, or correctly answering domain-specific questions that didn't appear in its training data. When you have examples of the exact behavior you want — and enough of them — fine-tuning is the right tool.

Fine-tuning doesn't replace prompting; it changes what "default" looks like. After fine-tuning, the behavior you want becomes the model's baseline, which reduces the prompt engineering burden on every subsequent call.
