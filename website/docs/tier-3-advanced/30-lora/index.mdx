## Why This Matters

Standard fine-tuning a 7B parameter model requires ~28GB of GPU RAM — more than most cloud GPUs and far beyond any consumer hardware. LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation) cuts that to 3–5GB, making fine-tuning accessible on a single A100 or even a consumer RTX 3090.

This isn't a compromise. LoRA-fine-tuned models match or exceed full fine-tuning quality on most tasks, train faster, and produce small adapter files (a few MB) rather than full model copies (14GB+). For organisations running multiple fine-tuned variants, adapters are also hot-swappable — one base model, many behaviours.
