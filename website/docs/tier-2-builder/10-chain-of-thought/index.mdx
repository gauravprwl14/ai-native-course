# Chapter 10 — Chain-of-Thought Prompting

## What You'll Learn

- What chain-of-thought (CoT) prompting is and why it works
- Zero-shot CoT: the single phrase that unlocks better reasoning
- Few-shot CoT: providing worked examples to guide the model
- When CoT helps — and when it is a waste of tokens
- Self-consistency: generating multiple reasoning chains and voting
- Limitations of CoT and how to work around them

## Prerequisites

- Chapter 09 (Zero/Few-shot Prompting) — you should understand how examples influence model behaviour
- Python 3.10+, `anthropic` package installed

## How This Connects

Chain-of-thought is the bridge between simple prompting and advanced reasoning systems. Once you understand CoT, you can see how agents (Chapter 20–21) use the same principle — generating intermediate steps — but in a loop. Structured output (Chapter 12) is often needed to cleanly extract the final answer from a CoT response.
